But back in the days, there were no circuits like this, there were no standards in the circuits made from these switches. In the 19th century, George Boole developed Boolean algebra, a mathematical system for representing logical statements using values like true and false.

Much later, in 1937, Claude Shannon realized in his master’s thesis that Boolean algebra could be directly applied to electrical switching circuits. This showed that combinations of switches could implement logical operations such as AND, OR, and NOT.

And in remembrance and honor of George Boole's theory of boolean algebra, the data type for true and false, 0 and 1, is named after, the boolean.
